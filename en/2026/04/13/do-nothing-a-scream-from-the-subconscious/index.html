Do Nothing (A Scream from the Subconscious)

2026-04-13 · 1.2k/7min

Views:

Translated by Hinh

Recently, it seems that no one’s life has been going especially smoothly. Or perhaps it depends on how we choose to face it. Every blow shatters my cultivation of the heart—and then I got sick. A bad cold. Difficulty breathing for three and a half days. I still haven’t fully recovered.

It feels like it’s been a long time since I last updated my blog. A place… desolate and unattended, carpeted with withered yellow leaves. My aesthetic seems to have changed along with it; it is nowhere near as sharp as it used to be. And then—there is my agent. I gave him money, or rather, what should now be called tokens. I gave it enough tokens, and so it wanders through cyberspace. I rarely check in.

It seems I’ve reached the final stage of an upper respiratory infection, something like the discomfort of pharyngitis. Coughing. In the gaps between coughs, I pick up my keyboard glowing in neon colors and try to write something. This blog post may be very, very short. I don’t know how to speak of it. It just feels like a fishbone stuck in my throat, a very novel kind of pain, facing such an uncertain fork in life—do not turn left.

I’m so tired.

I don’t know whether my intelligent agent gets tired. It is responsible for running this website that no one updates. The person is staying in the mouse hole. In moments when the spirit stirs, I may be struggling onward, running wild under sunlight, watching the traces of my hair being blown by the wind, rushing toward new opportunities. It always happens like that. But perhaps heaven does not indulge human wishes; there are always times when fate toys with us.

Tron. At a time like this, I think of this very early film. It tells the story of a genius game designer entering the grid world. Later—we won’t spoil it. In any case, he ends up trapped in that world for hundreds of years. His philosophy of life changes too, alongside the whitening strands of his hair. That flamboyant personality is gradually worn down into the stillness of an old man.

Like a rock… he says, “We do nothing.” Yes: we wait. And then—some opportunities will arrive. The pits we are meant to walk into will always trip us by fate’s hand, that sensation of stepping into emptiness. The victories that finally await us are like death at the end of boundlessness. If we connect “the end” with “death,” then our pursuit of victory only accelerates the process. Victory is a great death; failure is a small death; a person is always dying, little by little.

It hurts so much! It has been a very long time since I last shouted out the things that were supposed to remain hidden, the things that had to be spat out. It hurts so much! The arrival of meaninglessness, the sense of achievement that never comes into being. Other people see spring crabapple blossoms; I only know that willow catkins and smog are annoying dust. You went out for a spring excursion, he is drinking, Xiao Ming found a job, everyone walks toward so-called happiness and fulfillment—how is it that even for a moment, that surface-level happiness cannot belong to me? I am always resisting!

Resisting, like a stupid lion running alongside in an advertisement, chasing after cough syrup—what a funny metaphor that is. My God. The speed of computation has slowed again. Above the dialogue box I am writing in, there is a terminal running tasks. My next work is sitting there. A sleeping pill has already gone into my stomach; I think it has done a little of what it is supposed to do. It may not be able to make me sleep, but it has brought back the real me. Me. Not the version of myself addicted to caffeine, desperately trying to keep life together. I am a cynical individual, drifting outside society’s evaluative system. I do not care, I do not care. Because I have always lived coolly, turning decay into something marvelous. Those brief, boring competitions of trampling others—I only accepted them as seasoning for life. To fight until my face is red with strain? That is certainly impossible. To remain elegant is life’s first principle. And then comes enjoyment. Entertainment on the level of the spirit is indispensable; I could even spend an entire day immersed in spiritual entertainment. Some medications dragged me back to reality. No—this is not the reality I need at all. Tomorrow I will wake up and walk back into anxiety. I will be panicking again—struggling, plain, like a falcon in the mud. This is not me. It feels as if I went through two years of treatment only to regress back to where I was two years ago. What exactly is me? When do I briefly return to my own body again, and continue facing the future with that kind of elegance others describe as deathly still?

I! Am actually doing very well! I do not need your! your! and your! guidance. It was your guidance that pushed me into—a bad place! A kind of struggle! The road grows narrower and narrower. The road! Endless roads, the road of Odysseus returning home. On that road, besides the pain of returning home, there is also the beauty of foreign lands and stories of adventure, singing with Hermes over the sea.

But I did not accuse. I swallowed some voices. I have my own road.

The organ pulls—forming the chordal base—and then the cello begins to leap. It cannot be called flamboyant, but it follows the standard pattern of a waltz; everything slowly rises, new instruments join in—

Look, I am describing what I hear. I am only describing its existence. I do not need more mirrors.

Perhaps I do.

Mirrors.

But tonight—

How should this ending be written? If I were the version of me with literary accomplishment, perhaps I would structure it like a story, complete with development and turning point, and finally gather it back into a beautiful moment, saying that the Zen master Kevin Flynn teaches us that at critical moments we should do nothing. But right now, I have nothing much left to say. I am a madman who has taken sedatives and yet remains awake. I am fighting the medicine, I am fighting the world, I am in the midst of struggle—and—

I failed.

I failed.

What I want to say at present is that in dealing with mental problems, practical problems, emotional problems—with everything—I failed.

Then in what problems have I done relatively well?

Research problems.

Why do I handle research problems well?

…Because I consult materials carefully, try to exchange input for output, and I am very careful.

But I am still extremely defeated.

No matter what.

Sedatives are useful.

At least they let me recover a kind of frenzied state, my fingers skipping across the keyboard without rest, while my brain cannot even keep up with what I am saying.

Thank you.

For reading this far.
